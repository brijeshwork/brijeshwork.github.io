Booking Management System Design

Architectural patterns for booking systems — availability models, conflict handling, calendar integration and high-concurrency booking flows.

Requirements

  • Accurate availability checks and prevention of double-booking
  • Support time zones and recurring bookings
  • Low-latency booking confirmation (user experience)
  • High concurrency and consistency for inventory like rooms or slots

Architecture patterns

  • Optimistic locking with conflict resolution for low-latency flows
  • Pessimistic locking (database row locks) for critical resources
  • Distributed lock service (Redis Redlock) for cross-node consistency
  • Event-driven eventual consistency for calendar sync and notifications

Availability models

Represent availability as time slots or capacity units depending on domain. Pre-reserve inventory for a short TTL to prevent race conditions during checkout.

Scaling & data flow

  • Partition bookings by resource (hotel id, location) to distribute load
  • Use CQRS for separating read-optimized availability views from write paths
  • Batch processing for reconciliation and cleanup of orphaned reservations

Integrations & calendars

Integrate with external calendars (Google Calendar, Outlook) using webhooks and normalized event models. Implement exponential backoff + deduplication for webhook retries.

Conclusion

Choose an availability model that matches your domain and combine locking strategies with a resilient reconciliation pipeline to maintain consistency and user experience.
