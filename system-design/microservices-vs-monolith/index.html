Microservices vs Monoliths — Which Architecture Fits Your Project?

A practical comparison of monolithic and microservice architectures with guidance on trade-offs, team organization, deployment and scaling.

Summary

Monoliths are simpler to develop and deploy early; microservices improve modularity and independent scaling but add operational overhead. Choose based on team size, release cadence, and scaling needs.

Key trade-offs

  • Operational complexity vs deployment independence
  • Latency & distributed transactions vs single-process performance
  • Team ownership and release velocity

Recommendations

  • Start with a modular monolith; split into microservices when scaling and organizational needs demand it
  • Invest in observability, CI/CD and automation before breaking into many services
